    Hi William,

    I have now entered up most of the data you supplied in your email of the 11th September and find it all most interesting. The only thing you seem to have wrong is that George John Bunbury was the son of GWB Snr and Alice Maud Mary nee Stone.

    GWB Snr retired from the Army 11.4 1864 so it is unlikely he was in India after that time. He died 17.8.1898 when George John was 10 years old so it was not GWB Snr who sent his son to Canada. George John's mother did not die until 2.11.1910, (some seven years after GJB's departure for Canada) so it would appear likely that either she or her brother a Frederick W.S. Stone (a doctor) would have arranged for GJB's transportation to Alberta. FWS Stone is named as the executor in Alice Maud Bunbury's Will - proven in 1911, so he would have been in charge after that date. I have not seen this Will and it might be interesting to find out whether either GJB or his sister Kathleen Susan were beneficiaries. Maybe Woodville House was heavily mortgaged as I heve seen a papers relating to GWB Snr asking for favours from his cousin Kane Bunbury, a Colonel in the 7th Dragoon Guards who had inherited 6,000 acres of land in Ireland. Kane Bunbury was born 25.7.1777 and died in 9.11.1874. He and his elder brother Thomas never married and their mother was Catherine Kane who married William Bunbury 1744-1778 (check Turtle's write up) GWB Snr is known to have asked for financial assistance from KB.

    It does look as though GJB put up a fair sized boo-boo with his mother to get transported to Canada.

    For the history book, GWB Jnr married an Agnes Gertrude Cleary on 19.9.1904 a son Thomas Delessert Bunbury was born 30.9.1905. Agnes died from TB in 1909 and GWB Jnr married again an Elizabeth Graham in 1911.

    TDB did not get on with his step-mother and as soon as he was able signed on as a crew for a ship bound for Australia. Legend says GWB Jnr took TDB back to Ireland but he departed for a second time. He married a Bessie Mathews in 1927 and she died in 1928. In 1929 he married a Minnie G. Holohan and there was one son Gregory born in 1930 (he is still around). Then TDB went bush in 1935 and lived with an Ethel Minney, having changed his name to Thomas Delessert Burgess. Australian Army records confirm this. His youngest daughter Hazel, one of seven contacted me to enquire about Bunbury history as she still thought she was a Burgess. I tend to think there is a similarity between GJB and TDB, both akin to their father/grandfather.

    So GJB's father would never have known that he was farming in Canada.

    To answer your questions - a Will has to be submitted to a court for a magistrate to be satisfied that it is authentic and legal. Usually referred to as a Probate Court where a Will is proven.

    Never having been in Ireland I am not familiar with the Irish vernacular, but I would guess it is just referred to as Tipperary.

    Do you know the names and dates of birth of the following Bunburys.

    The two sons of Lawrence John Bunbury.
    The two sons of Robert (Bob) George Bunbury.
    The two sons of Cyril (Barney) Bunbury.
    DOB of Daniel Bunbury's son named Jerard.

    All these would have passed the Bunbury surname on.

    Hope this sorts out any misconceptions about GWB Snr.
    All the bestest,
    Peter.
